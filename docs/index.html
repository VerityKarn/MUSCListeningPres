Grew up in Cobleskill, NY

Culture = NYC + Hick

From Italy, we moved to Cobleskill, New York, because there was family there that offered a house. That was a whole debacle by itself, but the important thing is that this was where the majority of my cultural upbringing came from. The best way to describe the culture is a mix of NYC and rural culture, which we tended to afectionately call "Hick". We were definitely not people who could call themselves "true" New Yorkers, according to those snobs in the Big Apple, but what happened in NYC usually had a big effect in Upstate. We felt this most powerfully during the 9/11 attacks, where the distinction between Upstate and the City vanished. At that point, everyone was just a New Yorker, regardless of how close we were to the epicenter. But, Upstate, and especially Schoharie County, often tried to find its own identity that stood out from NYC. For Coby, and the wider Schoharie County, that was adopting elements from Southern life. There were actually stores nearby where you could go buy Confederate flags, and this was seen as normal. There's a joke I remember traveling around the area that went "Why was the toothbrush invented in Schoharie County? Because if it was invented anywhere else, it would be called the 'teethbrush'.". Honestly, it was an interesting mix.(d)

"Metal speaks of demons, therefore it is demonic"

So, focusing on the Metal Stereotype, there are several aspects to point towards that I believe is missed on most people's first impressions. Overall, it's a misunderstanding of metal expression(d), which is rooted in both technical and metaphorical musical language. (d) The technical expression of metal is focused on intense virtuosity, mostly through difficult and impressive guitar riffs, bass lines, and drum beats. It's a genre that elevates difficult to recreate sounds, which are usually made by going against musical convention. The ballad structure may be used, but it can often be expanded or warped into new styles that display musical talent that is hard to appreciate from a first viewing. This need to constantly push musical convention is often alienating, so an outsider might just hear the music as pure noise. It's a self feeding loop, where those who appreciate metal tend to be the "in-crowd", while outsiders are just stiffs who don't get it. Given that most metal fans tend to see themselves as outsiders to begin with, it's comforting, but definitely makes it harder to get people into the wider metal conversation. Add to that(d) metal's typical approach to dark and vague imagery to elucidate some deeper meaning, and we can see how the metaphorical aspect of metal gets tied into the mistranslation of the technical aspect. Demons, hell, fire, what have you, are regular images that appear in metal lyrics, and the first impression tends to come across as violent. However, this is where we run into (d) the problem of metaphor versus interpretation. Metaphor, in any medium, is only as good as it's ability to be interpreted. So, a song talking about summoning demons to slay one's enemies could run into a very basic problem. (d) "If it speaks of demons, it must actually mean demons." To a metal fan, the song about summoning demons is likely interpreted as "Bringing forward one's deep emotions to better attack the problems of reality". To an outsider, the song is literally, "I want to summon Hell to kill people." The difference between these interpretations are massive, and the latter was the main driving force for(d) the 1985 Tipper Gore hearings on Metal/Rock/Rap music and public safety. Though, ironically, that fueled each of those genres to become less mainstream. Funny, that.(d)

Her name is actually a reference to a thrash metal band from the 80s/90s called Meliah Rage, while she also has a demon in her hair called Angra, which is a metal band from Russia, the country Millia comes from. To explain that oddity, let's explore(d)

Millia's Story

  • Grew up in Assassin's Guild
  • Experimented on as a child
  • Questionable relationship with Zato=1
  • Became Director of PWAB
Millia's Story. Now, I'll state here that there are literally enough analyses of each of these characters to write a freaking book about. I'm going to try and give a quick enough overlook focusing on the relevant information for each character's theme to be understandable. If you have further questions on each character, check the wiki, or ask me later. There's a lot to unpack.

For Millia, (d)she started out as an orphan that was kidnapped by the Assassin's Guild, and then(d) used for human experimentation on forbidden magic to summon the forbidden demons. That's what led to her hair becoming a weapon. Alongside her was a guy named Zato-1, on the right, which is apparently a pun about a blind swordsman named Zatoichi. Kind of makes sense because he had to give up his sight to gain his demon Eddie, which is either a reference to Eddie Van Halen or the mascot for Iron Maiden, who's called Eddie the Head. Like I said, the evidence is not concrete. While they grew up, Millia and Zato started a relationship(d) which became destructive as time went on. The short version was that Eddie was violent, made Zato violent, abused Millia, she left then vowed to kill Zato, but then Eddie killed Zato, then Zato was resurrected and lost all his memories, but remembered that he cared about Millia, which she just said "fine" to, and now they are kind of co-workers, but also maybe thinking about getting back together? Trust me, this was the shortest way to describe that atom bomb of a relationship. Point is, Zato doesn't remember anything, and Millia is trying to figure out if their relationship could ever be fixed. While they were figuring that out, both of them rose up the ranks of the Assassin's guild, and eventually became the heads of (d) an organization known as the Post-War Administration Bureau. Don't ask, that's a whole other rabbit hole. Instead, let's focus on how all of this is explained through the theme itself.(d)

Main Ideas of Theme

  • Anger and Doubts about herself
  • Anger and Doubts about Zato
  • Must move forward regardless
Given her backstory, Millia is going through quite a bit internally, and most of it comes through in her theme. To begin with, (d)she never really got over the fact that she's technically possessed by a demon, which is the main point of the first verse, where she vents her anger and doubts about herself. She is also struggling with her relationship with Zato(d), which is the focus of the second verse. The chorus tells us how she's dealing with this situation(d), which is that she believes that she must move forward regardless. Whether these things consume her, or whether she can push through to reach the moonlight, Millia must learn to not only live with these problems, but accept them for what they are. Hence, why she must love the subhuman self.(d)

Main Musical Elements of Theme

  • Main Sound: Harsh, Fast, Angry Guitar and Electronics
  • Underlying Sound: Softer, Subtle Strings and Piano
  • Main Thematic Throughline: Unforgiving and angry
  • Underlying Thematic Throughline: Pleading and sad
Now, the Musical elements of Millia's theme are actually two fold. (d) There is the main sound, which is mostly taken up by harsh, fast, and angry guitar and sythetic instruments. Yet, underneath that(d) is a softer and subtle integration of string and piano music, which pops out to take the main stage at times, but is often swallowed up by the main sound shortly after. Musically(d), the main thematic throughline comes across as unforgiving and angry, but this sound is puncuated by the (d) subtler thematic throughline, which is the outcry of a soul in need of some comfort. I would recommend one listens to this song twice. For the first listen, I'd recommend just absorbing the main sound, while the second listen ought to focus on the subtler elements that lie beneath. As much as I tried to go for simple enough music, a lot of this is hard to hear with only one listen, which is why I split these elements into Main/Underlying sections.(d)

Song 2

Like A Weed, Naturally, as a Matter of Course

Theme of Testament

For the second song, I chose(d) Like A Weed, Naturally, as a Matter of Course, which is the theme song of Testament, the person in the background. They're named after the band Testament, which started in 1983 and is still making music today. One of their moves is called Unholy Diver, which is a reference to one of my favorite albums, Holy Diver by Dio. Anyway, let's move into understanding(d)

Testament's Story

  • Kidnapped and experimented on for genetic weapon projects
  • Possessed by Justice in order to destroy the world
  • Afterwards, tried to help Justice's innocent daughter, Dizzy
  • Spent the last 7 years in solitude to discover themself
Testament's Story. Again, I'll keep this short and relevant to the theme. So, at first Testament was an orphan. You'll notice that this is a regular pattern for backstories. They were found and raised by the leader of the Crusades, before eventually(d) being kidnapped and experimented on for genetic weapon projects called GEARs. Another regular pattern for backstories in GG. Eventually, the Command GEAR, Justice, rebeled and tried to wipe out humanity by controlling all the other GEARs in existence,(d) including Testament. What follows was the first two games of the series, where Justice is eventually defeated and Testament is freed from her control. Now straddled with a lifetime of guilt and confusion, Testament finds Justice's daughter, Dizzy, and(d) decides to try and protect her from the world. That story is GGX2, which is what the background art is from. Eventually, Dizzy leaves and Testament(d) decides to secluded themself from the world and rediscover their purpose. Testament's return to the series in Strive is after that long time in solitude, and the lessons they learned while away from everybody else.(d)

Main Ideas of Theme

  • All life is connected
  • Life is neither good or evil
  • Life is worth living for its own sake
  • Testament doesn't want to change the world, but observe it
The new image is Testament in Strive, 7 years after the events of GGX2. One thing to note moving forward is that part of that discovery was coming to terms with their gender identity, and they now identify as mu-gender, which is a Japanese term for a non-binary person who feels that they are both genders combined. Basically, they see themselves as both male and female. In the theme, you'll hear the song switch between the pronouns "he" and "she". That's the reason why. Beyond the identity aspect, Testament's theme tells us how their life philosophy has changed, and gives us a good look into the answers they found while in solitude. To start(d), Testament now sees all life as connected, and specifically themself as "one of a chain". They also(d) believe that life itself is neither good or evil. "It's just life". And because it's just life(d), it's worth living for its own sake, rather than some higher purpose. And because life is worth living for its own sake(d), Testament doesn't want to change the world, but simply observe it. Though, they're more than aware of how others view them as a potentially destructive force in the world.(d)

Main Musical Elements of Theme

  • Main Sound: Energetic, Rising Vocals, Guitar, and Strings
  • Underlying Sound: Guitar and Bass in Gallop Step
  • Main Thematic Throughline: Triumphant, Joyful Rising
  • Underlying Thematic Throughline: Fast but Grounded Gallop
As for the Musical Elements of the Theme, I ought to point out how Testament's theme utilizes the exact same set of instruments as Millia's theme, but how they are applied gives off two incredibly different senses of emotional connection. It's also like Millia's, in that I'd recommend at least two passthroughs, with each focused on the main throughline and the underlying throughline. For the main sound(d), there are Energetic and usually rising Vocals, Guitar and Strings. For the Underlying sound(d), there is the Guitar and Bass setting a pulsing rythmn in Gallop step. When I say pulsing, I mean there is a baseline note, with the instrument jumping up in pitch at regular intervals. These patterns appear often in Metal music, so recognizing both a Gallop and Pulse is helpful overall. Anyway, the main thematic throughline(d) is a triumphant, joyful rising, like their soul is taking flight. Underneath that(d) is the subtle throughline of a fast but grounded gallop. So while their soul is soaring, their perception is firmly in place and moving forward with their new perspective.(d)

Song 3

Drift

Theme of Happy Chaos

For the third song, it's time that we took a dip into the deep end of the pool. Taking the elements from the first two, now we can try to put together the weird side of metal, where the song's sense of time and direction is much harder to grasp.(d) For that, I've gone with Drift, which is(d) the theme of Happy Chaos(HC). And, funny enough, Happy Chaos is a perfect way to describe both the character and theme.(d)

Chaos' Story

  • Original Discoverer of Magic and the Backyard(higher dimension controlling the universe)
  • Secluded himself in the Backyard
  • "Stare into the abyss, and it stares back"
  • Began to believe that Chaos made better stories
  • Influenced events from the Backyard to create Chaos
So, who is HC? Well, if you thought the first two backstories were convoluted and hard to grasp, get ready, because it's time to get crazy. HC is actually(d) the original discoverer of Magic as a tenable force and science within the world of GG, and the Backyard, which is a higher dimension that is basically a computer running the actual universe that GG is set in. Yes, the story goes freaking meta at one point, and literally tells the characters that they are programmed simulations within a machine running the universe. While most of the characters tend to take this in stride and keep living their lives, HC was intruiged by the Backyard(d), and decided to seclude himself in this new dimension. From there, he began to see the GG universe as a story, and started to become entirely detached from it. There's actually several points where he directly points out the tropes of the Story Mode, and even plays right into them, just because that makes the story more engaging. Basically, HC has become a weird halfway point between the characters of the story, and us the audience, who sees the world from the outside. Personally, he almost seems like a criticism of people like me, who try way too hard to explain how GG works. Regardless(d), HC is basically the embodiment of "Stare into the abyss, and it stares back." His discovery forever changed him, and he's something that's almost impossible to explain. But, because he sees the events of the world of GG as a Story, he began to differentiate between what makes a good story and a bad story.(d) The answer that he came across was that a good story needs to push the characters to their limits. If a hero is not challenged, then the victory doesn't feel earned. And if it's too difficult, then the hero fails. So, how to make a story that pushes the limits, while also "hoping that you guys win"? Well(d), from the Backyard he began to influence the wider story in little ways. A slight tragedy here, and bit of misfortune there, and the world was placed in the perfect position to bring forward strong characters who made the story that much more interesting. Because the one thing HC wants is to avoid a boring story, even if that means he has to put himself in the middle of it. Again, he's weird. It's best to just not think about it too much.(d)

Main Ideas of Theme

  • The "Truth" is not the same as "Reality"
  • Everything is a story
  • The best stories are a violent storm followed by beautiful skies
  • No defined motivations, except to keep the story going forever
  • "Please don't stop the flow"
So, the main ideas of the theme, we can start with a doozy of an idea(d), that truth is not the same as reality. For GG, this makes sense, because there is literally a "real" universe that is controlled by another dimension, which means that their "reality" is not the "truth". And because the "truth" is not "reality"(d), then everything can be best explained and understood as a story. And the best stories(d) are those which are violent storms followed by beautiful skies. Said another way, the best stories are struggling and suffering followed by a calm and peaceful resolution. As for HC's motivation(d), he has none, except to keep the story going forever. Hence the line(d) "Please don't stop the flow", after he reaches his own calm seas. No matter what, he just keeps diving in, because it's far more interesting that way.(d)
